How intelligent is the octopus?

BBC Science · 2 August 2018 · 6 min read

Researchers at the Marine Biological Laboratory have discovered that octopuses possess a form of episodic memory - the ability to recall specific past events - long thought to be exclusive to mammals and birds.

The study, published in Current Biology, found that octopuses could remember the location, timing, and outcome of individual foraging events, adapting their future behaviour accordingly.

Nine brains, infinite curiosity

An octopus technically has nine brains - one central and one in each arm - each capable of independent decision-making. Scientists believe this distributed nervous system underpins their extraordinary adaptability.

Dr Yuki Tanaka noted that unlike social animals, octopuses are almost entirely solitary - meaning their cognitive complexity arose through a completely different evolutionary pathway to our own.
